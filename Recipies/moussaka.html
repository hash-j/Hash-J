Ingredients

For the Egg Plant

  • 2 large eggplants
  • Salt

For the meat sauce

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the béchamel sauce

  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 4 tablespoons flour
  • 3 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 3 eggs, separated
  • Nutmeg

Instructions

Prepare the eggplant

Slice the eggplant into 1/2-inch thick rounds. Salt the eggplant slices and let them drain in a colander for about 30 minutes to remove bitterness. Rinse and pat dry.

Make the meat sauce

Brown the ground beef in a large skillet. Add onion and garlic and cook until softened. Stir in crushed tomatoes, cinnamon, cloves, salt, and pepper. Simmer for about 30 minutes.

Make the béchamel sauce

Melt butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Add flour and cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly. Gradually whisk in the milk until smooth. Bring to a simmer and cook for 5 minutes, stirring constantly. Remove from heat and stir in Parmesan cheese and egg yolks. In a separate bowl, beat egg whites until stiff peaks form and gently fold into the béchamel sauce.

Assembling

Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). In a baking dish, layer the eggplant slices, meat sauce, and béchamel sauce. Repeat layers until all ingredients are used, ending with a layer of béchamel sauce. Sprinkle with nutmeg.

Bake the Moussaka

Bake for 30-40 minutes, or until golden brown and bubbly.

Tips

  • You can use ground lamb instead of beef, or a combination of both.
  • For a vegetarian option, omit the meat and add extra vegetables like mushrooms or zucchini.
  • Serve moussaka hot with a side salad.
